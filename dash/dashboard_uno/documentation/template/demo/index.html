Thermal
stress

 The temperature of 30°C is too high for cultivation, since
the optimal range is between 15°C and 20°C. Such high
temperatures can cause reduced tuber growth and
increase the risk of heat stress in plants. It is
recommended to increase irrigation to compensate
for evaporation caused by high temperatures,
but without saturating the soil.

Acceptable
humidity

 Regularly monitor soil moisture. If humidity is expected
to continue to decrease, it is recommended to increase
the frequency of watering to keep it at an adequate level
(around 60-80% for potatoes).

Low
precipitation

 Due to low precipitation, irrigation must be carried out
to meet the needs of the crop. The amount of water must
be calculated based on evapotranspiration and the
retention capacity of the soil.

Adequate
reflectance

 A reflectance of 15% can indicate that plants are
adequately capturing sunlight, which is positive
for photosynthesis.
